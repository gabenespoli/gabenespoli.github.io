MLB Data Description

PUBLISHED ON NOV 23, 2018

I have had a tough time finding this information online in one place, so I thought I would amalgamate it here. Many of the descriptions come from a Fast Balls article from 2007, but there have been many variables added since then. In most cases it was obvious so I’ve spelled out a description.

Variable Name Description
index A unique identification number per pitch within a game. The numbers increment by one for each pitch but are not consecutive between at bats.
pitch_type* AB: Automatic Ball
AS: Automatic Strike
CH: Change-up
CU: Curveball
EP: Eephus
FC: Cutter
FF: Four-Seam Fastball
FO: Forkball
FS: Splitter
FT: Two-Seam Fastball (synonymous with SI)
GY: Gyroball
IN: Intentional Ball
KC: Knuckle Curve
KN: Knuckleball
NP: No Pitch
PO: Pitchout
SC: Screwball
SI: Sinker (synonymous with FT)
SL: Slider
UN: Unknown
game_date Date the game was played (yyyy-mm-dd)
release_speed The pitch speed, in miles per hour and in three dimensions, measured at the initial point, y0. Of the two speeds, this one is closer to the speed measured by a radar gun and what we are familiar with for a pitcher’s “velocity” .
release_pos_x The left/right distance, in feet, of the pitch, measured at the initial point.
release_pos_z The height, in feet, of the pitch, measured at the initial point.

pfx_x The horizontal movement, in inches, of the pitch between the release point and home plate, as compared to a theoretical pitch thrown at the same speed with no spin-induced movement. This parameter is measured at y=40 feet regardless of the y0 value.
pfx_z The vertical movement, in inches, of the pitch between the release point and home plate, as compared to a theoretical pitch thrown at the same speed with no spin-induced movement. This parameter is measured at y=40 feet regardless of the y0 value.
plate_x The left/right distance, in feet, of the pitch from the middle of the plate as it crossed home plate. The PITCHf/x coordinate system is oriented to the catcher’s/umpire’s perspective, with distances to the right being positive and to the left being negative.

sv_id A date/time stamp of when the PITCHf/x tracking system first detected the pitch in the air, it is in the format YYMMDD_hhmmss.
vx0 The horizontal velocity of the pitch measured at the initial point, in feet per second.
vy0 The forward (towards home plate) velocity of the pitch measured at the initial point, in feet per second.
vz0 The vertical velocity of the pitch measured at the initial point, in feet per second.
ax The horizontal acceleration of the pitch measured at the initial point, in feet per second per second.
ay The forward (towards home plate) acceleration of the pitch measured at the initial point, in feet per second per second.
az The vertical acceleration of the pitch measured at the initial point, in feet per second per second.
sz_top The distance in feet from the ground to the top of the current batter’s rulebook strike zone as measured from the video by the PITCHf/x operator. The operator sets a line at the batter’s belt as he settles into the hitting position, and the PITCHf/x software adds four inches up for the top of the zone.
sz_bot The distance in feet from the ground to the bottom of the current batter’s rulebook strike zone. The PITCHf/x operator sets a line at the hollow of the knee for the bottom of the zone.
